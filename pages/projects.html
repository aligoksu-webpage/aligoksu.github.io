Ongoing (PhD)

Dual Function Materials for Integrated CO2 Capture and Utilisation

University of Surrey | 2022 - Present

Research focused on ambient pressure CO2 hydrogenation to methanol and C1 products. The project involves hands-on expertise with XRD, SEM, Micro-GC, Mass Spectrometry, Gas Analysers, DSC-TGA, and catalyst testing. The goal is to develop materials that can both capture carbon dioxide and catalyse its conversion into useful fuels.

Electro-reduction of CO2 to Formic Acid

Columbia University | 2012 - 2014

Conducted research on the electro-reduction of Carbon Dioxide to formic acid using lead electrodes. This project investigated the electrochemical pathways and efficiency of converting greenhouse gases into value-added chemicals.

Columbia University | 2012 - 2014

Investigated the passivation of lead for lead-acid battery systems. The study focused on understanding the electrochemical behavior of lead surfaces to improve battery performance and longevity.
